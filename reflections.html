January 1, 2014

A new year. This last year for me was one of learning to trust myself and overcoming my own obstacles. A year spent becoming me again. I had loose ends to tie up, ducks to put in rows, surgery had and body mended. Along the way I found my passions: coding and community.

Coding is creating. It's building, it's problem-solving, and it can make amazing things. The internet and the world of connected things, has the opportunity to create community in a world where we are discarding the old ways we created community. It gives people ways to connect with like-minded people from all over the world. It gives people ways to connect to their governments in ways that weren't possible 20 years ago. I hope to learn and use my skills to increase community and increase civic engagement on an every-day level.

Learning to code has been a lot of fun. Challenging at times, but that adds to the fun. The past 6 months I have immersed myself in learning these new languages. I've taken online classes in through Codecademy, which I highly recommend to anyone wanting to see what it's all about. That whetted my appetite for more. I took local classes in Beginning and Intermediate HTML and CSS, as well as JavaScript, through Girl Develop It. GDI offers affordable classes to women in many cities around the world, usually taught by women. It was a great way for me to dive in without feeling intimidated. I also learned that I learn best in a group environment. I then signed up for a weekend workshop on Ruby on Rails offered free to women by The Railsbridge Group here in Seattle. That was a LOT of fun, and I met some amazing women. I took a Git workshop through NIRD, also free, also in Seattle. We have SO MANY resources available to us now.

I signed up with Edmonds Community College for their new PACE-IT program in web development. It's self-paced and online and tuition-free. The program is just starting and has a lot of kinks to be worked out. So far the most valuable aspect has been a mentor that calls weekly and helps with goal-setting and keeping me on track. Hopefully there will be internships available to students as well, which would be very valuable.

I realized that I needed more information than the EDCC program was providing, so I went ahead and signed up for Treehouse, which does cost money, but is well-worth the fee for more advanced classes.

My goal for 2014 is to have a job in web development. To work hard and laugh more.

Happy New Year!
